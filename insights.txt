Insight 1 - from Q1 or Q5 (category funding)
Which category received the highest funding, and what does it suggest about investor interest?

Biotech received the highest total funding at around $66.87B, contributing about 16.19% of total recorded funding. Software had more funded companies, but biotech attracted larger funding amounts overall. This shows that looking only at company count can be misleading. For business analysis, funding amount and number of funded companies should be compared together before deciding which category is performing strongest.

Insight 2 - from Q3 or Q8 (yearly trend / YoY change)
What pattern did funding show across the years? Was there any major rise or fall?

Funding increased strongly after 2004 and stayed high through 2006 to 2008. It dropped in 2009, then reached its highest point in 2010 at around $55.19B. This suggests that funding activity was affected by wider market conditions, but the recovery was quick in the following year. A business analyst should not treat one weak year as a category problem without checking the broader timeline.

Insight 3 - from Q2 or Q7 (top companies and country concentration)
Which companies or countries dominate the funding data, and what does that tell us?

The highest funded companies are concentrated mostly in the USA, with Clearwire, Verizon Communications, and sigmacare leading the list. The USA also has the highest number of funded companies by a large margin, followed by countries like GBR, CAN, FRA, ISR, DEU, and IND. This shows that the dataset is useful for comparing startup funding patterns, but country-level concentration should be considered before making global conclusions.
