Insight 1 — from Q1 or Q5 (category revenue)
Which category drove the most revenue and by how much? What does that suggest the business should do (stock more, promote more, focus ad spend)?

Electronics was the top revenue-generating category, contributing 43% of total revenue — nearly ₹6,000 more than the next category, Accessories. The business should ensure consistent stock availability for Electronics to meet sustained demand. Simultaneously, lower-performing categories like Fitness and Fashion need targeted promotions to improve their revenue share and reduce over-dependence on a single category.

Insight 2 — from Q3 or Q8 (monthly trend / MoM change)
What pattern did you see across months? Any spike or dip? What could explain it from a business perspective?

Revenue peaked in January 2023 and declined majorly through February, recovering gradually before rising again in July — which recorded the highest mid-year revenue. The second half showed relative stability but never returned to January levels, closing the year at ₹3,000+. This pattern suggests a "possible" seasonal demand cycle or a January effect that wasn't sustained. The business should investigate whether discounting or campaign timing strategies drove the January and July peaks, and do the same moreover again to replicate those conditions.

Insight 3 — from Q2 or Q7 (customer behaviour)
Who are the high-value or repeat customers? What does that tell the business about retention or loyalty?

Arjun Mehta, Priya Nair, and Ananya Singh were the highest-value repeat customers of the year. Only 50% of the customer base placed more than one order, indicating a retention problem — the business is acquiring customers but not bringing them back. A post-purchase follow-up strategy targeting these one-time buyers could significantly improve repeat purchase and lifetime value and their loyalty.
